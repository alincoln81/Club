History of the 86

The number 86 has a significant role in Toyota's chassis codes, used for identifying specific models. One iconic example is the AE86, known as the Hachi-Roku in Japan. It was a rear-wheel-drive high-performance coupe with a 1.6-liter 16-valve engine, known for its motorsport success.

The GT86, developed as a successor to the AE86, marked Toyota's return to rear-wheel drive and natural aspiration. It featured a 2.0-liter boxer engine and a driver-focused design, drawing inspiration from classic sports cars like the Toyota 2000GT.

In 2022, Toyota introduced the GR86, the third model in its GR lineup, which includes the GR Supra and GR Yaris. The GR86 maintains the fun-to-drive characteristics of the GT86 and offers a 2.4-liter boxer engine and improved torsional rigidity for enhanced handling.

The article highlights the significance of the number 86 in Toyota's performance engine bore diameter and stroke length. Engines like the 3S-GTE, 2JZ-GTE, and 4U-GSE have bore and stroke measurements of 86mm.

Overall, the number 86 has played a pivotal role in Toyota's performance models, from the AE86 to the GT86 and the new GR86, symbolizing a commitment to driver-focused, rear-wheel-drive sports cars.
